Education –A fundamental right for a prosperous nation



Illiteracy, ignorance and geographic inaccessibility are three major contributors for low human development index in the tribal dominated districts. Primary education is the fundamental right of every children and it is the window to the outside world which ensure a better and enlightened life, increases their employability and ensures their access to services and a better citizenry. Free and compulsory education is the fundamental right of every children under 14. Education not only makes children better and responsible citizens but also, they learn ethical, humane and moral values. Learned and educated person contribute significantly to national building.

THD considers education a window for every child to explore his creativity, identity and future prospect. Lack of access to quality education has always been a barrier of growth and development. Even as we evaluate the present education scenario in the State, student dropout cases is not encouraging at all. Though there has been substantial increase in enrollment, but the retention rate is alarmingly low. Almost half of the students enrolled in upper primary schools drop out by the time they complete high school education. The overall dropout rate in high schools for year 2011-12 has been estimated at 49.5%. This rate is much higher in backward areas like Kalahandi. Our own experience shows that decline in dropout rate at primary and upper primary levels are largely due to non-detention policy at the cost of quality education. Similarly, lack of strong foundation at primary and upper primary levels, among other factors, is a major contributing factor for slow rate of decline in retention at the secondary level as students are unable to cope with the pressures and the demands of secondary education. The state of Odisha continues to be a major contributor of child labor despite the fact that India is signatory to international treaty on Child Rights as well as our Constitution guarantees free and compulsory education as a fundamental right of every children in the age group of 6-14 years. The children who drop out at various stages of education end up as child laborers either at home settings or outside.

The other factor contributes for the low literacy rate and high dropout rate in the context of Kalahandi district is less numbers of teachers in school in comparison to the students. There is also scarcity of quality teachers in the Kalahandi district.
