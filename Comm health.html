Community Health and Nutrition – Health be not a burden



Odisha is infamous for its multifarious features of underdevelopment and symptoms of endemic poverty. Basic services such as community health, education, water and sanitation are distant dreams for the dwellers of rural areas due to its geographical inaccessibility, underdevelopment and socio-economic condition of the people. Government hospitals and clinics are irregular and insufficient. Community health assumes greater importance for transforming the socioeconomic living standard of the communities. Thus THD brings community health into the preview of its intervention for bringing about sustainable livelihoods and socio economic transformation in the lives of the communities. Considering the health situation in the region THD is committed to develop model villages where communities have better access to health care, water and sanitation, education, active village governance system to monitor and mobilize resources for a holistic development.

Nutrition is another important aspect of THD’s community health intervention. Because, most of the commonly occurred diseases are caused by insufficient immune system. Moreover, as rural residents are mostly physical workers, nutrition is very essential component in their day to day lives, given their socio-economic status. THD undertakes various awareness generation programs, street plays, sensitisation workshops for ASHA, AWW and ANMs to promote nutritional food among the target groups.

Insuring the poor through Biju Krushak Kalyan Yojna (BKKY) and RashtriyaSwasthyaBima Yojana (RSBY) is an annual programme THD undertakes every year which provides financial security to the vulnerable sections of the society, especially the BPLs. Although the scheme has been effective since 2010, results are not upto the mark. Lack of awareness among those who hold cards, reluctance on the part of hospitals made it imperative that the initiative might take a programmatic mode for successful and sustained effects. THD launched the programme in association with district labour dept, health dept and registered hospitals, while at the grassroot level several awareness generation programmes taken up. Apart from organizing sensitization camps at village, GP, Block and District level, cultural programmes on the theme conducted to woo beneficiaries. Individuals and functionaries associated to health sector such as AWW, ANM, PRI members, ASHA Workersand Sarpanchs are brought under the fold.
